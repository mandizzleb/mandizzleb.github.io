House of Balloons marks the debut mixtape from Canadian singer The Weeknd. It was released as a free download on March 21, 2011, by XO. Its music incorporates electronic and urban genres, including R&B and soul, along with trip hop, indie rock and dream pop tones with production from Canadian record producers such as Doc McKinney, Zodiac and Illangelo, among others. It is regarded as one of the best R&B mixtapes of all time and one of the best projects overall since 2010. This project is is a personal favourite for many of The Weeknd's hardcore fans, I'll be reviewing some of the standout tracks.

What You Need

Uploaded to YouTube in Dec. 2010, "What You Need" was the first Weeknd song many of us heard. The Aaliyah-sampling instrumental, cold and calm, is intriguing, but it's the Weeknd's delivery that stands out. Instead of being front and center, his lyrics are wrapped up nicely in the beat; they travel through the track like a ghost, like you're catching pieces of him singing into the wind. He pours his heart out about being better for his love than the zero she's with. While others might get wrapped up in the Xan'd out Weeknd, "What You Need" embodies the hopeless romantic at the project's core.
